The Enigma of Mona Lisa

Mona Lisa: The Masterpiece of Leonardo da Vinci

The Mona Lisa, painted by Leonardo da Vinci in the early 16th century, remains one of the most celebrated artworks in history. This portrait, believed to be of Lisa Gherardini, has captivated audiences for centuries with its remarkable technique and mysterious allure.

Quick Facts

  • Created between 1503-1519
  • Oil on poplar panel
  • Dimensions: 77 cm × 53 cm (30 in × 21 in)
  • Currently housed in the Louvre Museum, Paris

The Unforgettable Smile

The subject's expression is perhaps the most defining feature of the painting, often debated by art critics and historians. Some interpret it as a subtle smirk, while others see a deep and knowing gaze. The smile appears most prominent when viewed peripherally rather than directly, creating an ever-changing, elusive quality. Leonardo employed sfumato, a technique that creates soft transitions between colors and tones, giving the painting its lifelike appearance. This technique involves the layering of thin coats of paint to create a hazy, smoke-like effect (the word "sfumato" comes from the Italian word for smoke). The detailed textures and balanced composition highlight the artist's unmatched skill.
