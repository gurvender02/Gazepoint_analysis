Cloud Computing

Cloud computing provides on-demand access to computing resources over the internet. It removes the need for organizations to manage physical servers by offering virtualized services. The key service models are IaaS, PaaS, and SaaS, enabling varying levels of control and management.

Service and Deployment Models

IaaS delivers virtual machines and storage, exemplified by AWS EC2 and Google Compute Engine. PaaS offers development platforms like Heroku, abstracting server management. SaaS provides complete applications such as Office 365. Deployment can be public, private, hybrid, or community-based, each balancing cost, control, and security.

Did you know?

Amazon Web Services was launched in 2006 as S3 and EC2, kickstarting the modern cloud era.

Benefits and Challenges

Benefits include scalability, cost efficiency, and high availability. Auto-scaling adapts to traffic spikes, while CDNs reduce latency. Challenges involve data sovereignty, vendor lock-in, and ensuring compliance with regulations like GDPR and HIPAA.
